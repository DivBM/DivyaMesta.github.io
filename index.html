Title: Predictive and Therapeutic Assistive Model for Detection of Chronic Wound

Description: Chronic wounds affects millions of patients around the world and their treatment is challenging as the early signs indicating their development are subtle. In addition, a type of chronic wound, known as pressure ulcer develops in patients with limited mobility. Infection and frequent bleeding are indicators of chronic wound development. In this work, we present a model realized through prediction algorithm which compares the parameters like variations in pH levels and temperature at wound site. Status of the wound can be determined based on the prediction and medication can be provided accordingly. This work is an important step towards futuristic wearable sensors for remote health care applications.
